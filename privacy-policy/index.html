Privacy Policy

This policy explains how SteadyDesk IT handles information submitted through our website, support inquiries, and business collaboration requests.

Last updated: July 21, 2026Applies to steadydeskit.com

On this page

Scope Information we collect How we use information When information is shared Retention and security Your privacy rights Contact us
Website privacy at a glance

We use inquiry details to respond to you and operate our business. We do not sell personal information, and we do not currently use the site for targeted advertising or significant-decision profiling. Please never send passwords, recovery keys, or verification codes through a website form or ordinary email.

1. Who we are and what this policy covers

SteadyDesk IT Services LLC ("SteadyDesk IT," "we," "us," or "our") provides practical managed IT support for small businesses. This Privacy Policy applies to personal information handled through steadydeskit.com, including requests for technical support, service information, advertising, collaboration, and general contact.

This policy describes our website practices. If you become a client, a proposal, service agreement, statement of work, data-processing agreement, or other signed document may contain additional privacy, confidentiality, access, and security terms. Those contract-specific terms govern the applicable services.

2. Information we collect

Information you submit

Our contact forms may collect your name, business email address, company name, inquiry type, message, and confirmation that we may use the details to respond. If you contact us about advertising or collaboration, your message may also describe your audience, campaign, project, goals, budget, timing, or requested services.

Technical and operational information

Our hosting, security, content-delivery, and email systems may automatically process limited technical data needed to deliver and protect the site. Depending on the provider and request, this may include an IP address, date and time, requested page, referring page, browser or device information, HTTP request details, error records, and security events.

Information involved in client support

The public website form is only an intake channel. If you engage us for remote support, device management, business email, cloud backup, network support, or cybersecurity services, we may need additional information such as authorized user details, device or account identifiers, configuration information, diagnostic logs, support history, and limited access to business systems. We request and use that information only after appropriate authorization and under the applicable service documents.

Do not submit credentials through this website. Passwords, one-time codes, recovery keys, private encryption keys, payment-card numbers, health information, or other highly sensitive data should be shared only through an approved secure method when specifically requested.

3. How and why we use information

We may use information to:

  • receive, review, route, and respond to your inquiry;
  • understand a technical issue and determine whether we can help;
  • prepare a tailored service, advertising, or collaboration proposal;
  • schedule consultations, onboarding, projects, or support after agreement;
  • deliver authorized services and maintain related business records;
  • operate, troubleshoot, secure, and prevent misuse of the website;
  • protect our rights, users, clients, systems, and business; and
  • meet legal, accounting, tax, insurance, and compliance obligations.

Legal grounds where applicable

Depending on your location and the context, we rely on your consent, steps taken at your request before entering a contract, performance of a contract, compliance with law, and our legitimate interests in responding to business inquiries, operating securely, and keeping appropriate records. You may withdraw consent for future communications, but withdrawal does not affect processing already completed or information we must retain for another lawful reason.

4. When we disclose information

We do not sell personal information. We also do not currently share personal information for cross-context behavioral or targeted advertising. We may disclose information only as reasonably necessary to:

  • website hosting, email delivery, security, backup, and other vendors working for us;
  • authorized technicians or subcontractors supporting a requested service;
  • lawyers, accountants, insurers, and other professional advisers;
  • government authorities or other parties when required by law or reasonably necessary to protect rights, safety, and systems; or
  • a successor or potential successor in a merger, financing, reorganization, acquisition, or sale of business assets, subject to appropriate confidentiality protections.

Service providers are expected to use information only for the work they perform for us and to protect it appropriately. Third-party products that a client chooses to use, such as Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, cloud platforms, internet providers, or hardware vendors, operate under their own privacy terms.

5. Cookies and browser storage

The site currently uses local browser storage to remember the privacy choice you make in our cookie banner. We do not currently run analytics or advertising trackers. Hosting and security providers may still process ordinary request data needed to deliver and protect the site. See our Cookie Policy for the current technology list and instructions for changing your choice.

6. Retention and security

We retain information only for as long as reasonably needed for the purpose for which it was collected. Relevant factors include the status of your inquiry, whether you become a client, the need to preserve support or proposal history, legal and accounting requirements, dispute periods, security needs, and our ability to establish or defend legal claims. When information is no longer needed, we delete it, anonymize it, or securely isolate it until deletion is practical.

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to the nature of the information and our services. Measures may include access restrictions, secure communication methods, vendor oversight, backups, updates, monitoring, and incident procedures. No website, email system, remote connection, or storage method can be guaranteed completely secure, so please use the approved secure channel we provide for sensitive service information.

7. Your privacy rights and choices

Depending on where you live and whether a particular privacy law applies, you may have the right to:

  • confirm whether we process your personal information and obtain access to it;
  • correct inaccurate personal information;
  • request deletion of personal information;
  • receive a portable copy of information you provided;
  • opt out of a sale, targeted advertising, or certain profiling; and
  • appeal a decision about a privacy request or use an authorized agent where permitted.

We do not currently sell personal information, use it for targeted advertising, or use it to make legally significant decisions through profiling. To make another privacy request, email us using the address below and describe the request and the information or interaction involved. We may ask for information reasonably necessary to verify your identity and authority. We will not discriminate against you for exercising an applicable privacy right.

If we deny an appealable request, you may reply with the subject line "Privacy Appeal" and explain why you believe the decision should be reconsidered. You may also contact the appropriate state regulator where applicable.

8. Children

This is a business-to-business website and is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from a child under 13 through the site. If you believe a child has submitted information, contact us so we can review and delete it where appropriate.

9. United States processing and visitors from elsewhere

SteadyDesk IT is based in Texas, and website information may be processed and stored in the United States by us and our service providers. If you access the site from another country, your information may be transferred to a jurisdiction with different privacy rules. Where required, we use appropriate contractual or other safeguards for such transfers.

10. Third-party links and resources

The site may link to external websites or load limited resources from third-party infrastructure. Their privacy practices are controlled by their own policies, not this one. Review those terms before submitting information to an external service.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy when our services, website technology, vendors, or legal obligations change. The "Last updated" date identifies the current version. Material changes may also be highlighted on the site or communicated through another reasonable method when required.

12. Contact us

For privacy questions or requests, contact SteadyDesk IT Services LLC at support@steadydeskit.com or write to 201 W 5th Street, Suite 1100, Austin, TX 78701. Please use "Privacy Request" in the subject line and do not include passwords or other credentials.
