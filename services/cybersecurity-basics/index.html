Practical protection

Small, understandable defenses your team can follow every day.

Cybersecurity works better when employees understand the purpose of each safeguard. We focus on high-value basics—safer accounts, current devices, protected email, recoverable files, and a clear first response—without turning normal work into a maze.

A suspicious email reaches the team

Employees learn where to pause, what warning signs matter, and how to report a message without forwarding harmful links or attachments.

A password is reused or exposed

Password-manager habits, multi-factor authentication, and recovery controls reduce the value of a stolen password and improve account ownership.

A device looks compromised

The team knows whom to contact, when to disconnect, what not to delete, and which facts help a technician contain and investigate the issue.

Security baseline

The four areas we strengthen first.

We prioritize controls that reduce common small-business risk and are realistic to maintain. Recommendations are explained in business terms, assigned to an owner, and documented for follow-up.

Accounts and identity

Multi-factor authentication, unique credentials, recovery options, admin roles, and dormant accounts receive focused attention.

Devices and updates

Supported operating systems, encryption, protection status, screen locking, updates, and local administrator use are reviewed.

Email and domain

Delivery-authentication records, suspicious-message handling, mailbox forwarding, and administrator protections help reduce impersonation risk.

Response and recovery

A short action plan identifies first contacts, containment steps, important providers, backup status, and decision-makers before an incident.
